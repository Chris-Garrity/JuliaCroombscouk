About me

In 2008 I decided to embark on a career change and in 2009 I qualified with an ITEC Diploma in Holistic Massage (human) from Newbury College.

I have always had a passion for horses, riding since I was 10 years of age, so embarked upon an ITEC Diploma in Equine Sports Massage, under the renowned Mary Bromiley FCSP, SRP, RPT (USA) and completed this in 2010.

Since then I have been building both sides of the business, both human and equine and left my full time role in HR in December 2010, after 16 years, to fully concentrate on massage.

It gives me the greatest satisfaction when a client tells me the difference they have seen in their horse both in temperament and ridden work.

I am a full member of the Equine Sports Massage Association (ESMA) and fully insured through Holistic Insurance Services.

Who is ESMA?

ESMA (Equine Sports Massage Association) was set up as a Nationally recognised organisation to ensure the client receives services from a fully trained and qualified Equine Sports Masseur.

ESMA is the product of Mary Bromiley, she had the foresight to see the many benefits of Equine Massage and the importance of a governing body. The successful students on Mary's first ITEC accredited Equine Sports Massage Course developed the association and since then ESMA has grown from strength to strength.

Only students who have completed the ITEC Diploma in Equine Sports Massage, under Mary Bromiley, can become a member of ESMA and must complete hours of continual training and development.

Mary Bromiley, FCSP, SRP, RPT (USA) has been a practising chartered physiotherapist for over 35 years. She is world renowned for her outstanding knowledge in this field. Mary was the official physiotherapist for both horse and rider at the Olympics and World Championships for the New Zealand Three Day Event Team.
